Pod Assembly Fit

Precision integration for high-speed systems

Assembled and verified complex CAD components in Onshape and SolidWorks to ensure perfect alignment within the Hyperloop pod chassis. Addressed tolerance stack-up challenges in a real-world build environment.

What I Did

  • Verified CAD interface points for over 50 custom machined components.
  • Managed tolerance stack-up analysis to ensure sub-1mm alignment across the 2m chassis.
  • Executed final mechanical assembly and torque verification for safety-critical fasteners.

Key Decisions

  • Prioritized critical mating surfaces (brakes/propulsion) for tighter tolerances while loosening others to reduce cost.
  • Selected laser tracker metrology to validate the frame weldment before final assembly.

Results / Validation

  • Achieved successful integration of braking and propulsion subsystems without on-site rework.
  • Validated chassis structural integrity under simulated load conditions.

E-Bike Speed Control

Physics-based simulation for energy optimization

Designed a PID controller in C++ to manage e-bike speed under varying load conditions. Modeled air drag, rolling resistance, and incline disturbances to predict energy usage and system efficiency.

What I Did

  • Implemented a discrete-time PID control loop in C++ from scratch (no libraries).
  • Modeled system dynamics including aerodynamic drag and rolling friction forces.
  • Created a Python visualization script to plot velocity response and error over time.

Key Decisions

  • Implemented anti-windup clamping to handle actuator saturation during hill climbs.
  • Used a simplified point-mass model for real-time simulation performance.

Results / Validation

  • Maintained target velocity within ±0.5 m/s under varying incline disturbances.
  • Optimized controller gains (Kp, Ki, Kd) for minimal overshoot and settling time.

Carbon Fiber Components

Composite manufacturing for lightweight structures

Executed hand lay-up and vacuum bagging processes for custom carbon fiber parts. Optimized ply orientation for structural rigidity while minimizing weight for competitive vehicle applications.

What I Did

  • Performed wet lay-up of carbon fiber plies using epoxy resin systems.
  • Managed the vacuum bagging process to ensure proper consolidation and void removal.
  • Trimming and finishing of cured parts for final assembly fitment.

Key Decisions

  • Optimized ply orientation (0°/90° vs ±45°) based on expected load paths.
  • Used specific release agents to ensure mold longevity and surface finish quality.

Results / Validation

  • Produced lightweight aerodynamic shells with high stiffness-to-weight ratio.
  • Visual inspection confirmed absence of dry spots or delamination.
